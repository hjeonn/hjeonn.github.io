abstract: "Continuous monitoring of workers&#39; emotional states is essential for occupational health and safety, as emotional stress is directly linked to workplace accidents and long-term well-being. Traditional emotion recognition systems often rely on discrete categorical models or CNNs that struggle to capture the continuous nature of affect and long-range spatial dependencies in facial expressions. This paper presents a Vision Transformer (ViT)-based framework for continuous emotion estimation, specifically targeting industrial safety applications. The proposed approach utilizes a pre-trained ViT architecture, fine-tuned with an integrated regression head to simultaneously predict Valence and Arousal values. By leveraging the [CLS] token representation, the model captures global contextual relationships within facial features, addressing the limitations of local-feature-oriented architectures. Experimental validation on the JAFFE and CK+ datasets demonstrates robust performance, achieving RMSE values as low as 0.110 for Valence and 0.104 for Arousal. Our framework consistently outperforms state-of-the-art methods, including EmoNet and AVCE-FER, by margins of up to 33.7%. These results indicate that transformer-based architectures provide an effective solution for real-time worker stress monitoring and proactive safety management in smart industrial environments."
